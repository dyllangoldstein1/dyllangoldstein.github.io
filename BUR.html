Bruin Underwater Robotics (BUR)

Designing and building an Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV) for international competition.

[Image of an autonomous underwater vehicle]

Project Overview

As a member of Bruin Underwater Robotics, I am part of a team developing an Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV) to compete in the annual RoboSub competition. My role on the electrical sub-team involves designing the core electronic systems that power and control the vehicle.

Learning Objectives & Key Challenges

The primary engineering challenge is designing electronics that can operate reliably in a harsh underwater environment. This includes developing a robust power distribution system, waterproofing all enclosures and connectors, and ensuring clean power delivery to sensitive components like the main computer and sensors.

Anticipated Outcome & Skills Gained

The objective is to field a competitive AUV capable of autonomously navigating and completing its mission. This project provides invaluable experience in power electronics, battery management, and the system-level integration of sensors, motors, and computing hardware in a complex robotic system.

Technical Details

  • Team: Bruin Underwater Robotics
  • Role: Electrical Sub-team Member
  • Key Concepts:
    • Power Distribution Systems
    • Battery Management (BMS)
    • Motor Control (ESCs)
    • Waterproofing Electronics
