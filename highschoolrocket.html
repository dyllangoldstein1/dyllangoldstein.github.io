Early Explorations: Model Rocketry

A foundational project exploring the principles of aerospace engineering.

Project Overview

This project was my first hands-on introduction to engineering principles, undertaken during high school. It involved the complete process of designing, constructing, and successfully launching several model rockets, which solidified my interest in pursuing a career in engineering.

Design & Learning Process

The core of the project was understanding the physics of flight stability. I learned how to calculate the Center of Pressure (CP) and Center of Gravity (CG) for my designs, ensuring the CG was forward of the CP for a stable trajectory. This involved careful selection of materials and precise construction of the body, nose cone, and fins.

Outcome & What I Learned

Through numerous successful launches and recoveries, this project transformed my theoretical knowledge of physics into a tangible and exciting application. It was a foundational experience that taught me the importance of careful design, iterative testing, and the satisfaction of seeing an engineering project come to life.
