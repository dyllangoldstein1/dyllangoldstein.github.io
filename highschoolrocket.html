Early Explorations: Model Rocketry

The beginning of my passion for engineering.

Project Overview

This project was my first hands-on engineering experience. Competing in The American Rocketry Challenge, I captained my high school team in building several competition rockets with the goal altitude and flight time changing every year.

Design & Learning Process

Working on these rockets taught me the core principles of design as I worked in OpenRocket to simulate varying rocket designs. Eventually once a material, height, engine, and fin count was settled on, I got to create and mentor others on the assembly of a model rocket.

Outcome & What I Learned

Through numerous successful launches and recoveries, this project transformed my knowledge of physics into something both tangible and exciting. It was the experinece that led me to want to pursue engineering and continue working on projects throught my education and career.

Technical Details

  • Role: Student Designer & Builder
  • Tools:
    • RockSim (or similar)
    • Standard workshop tools
  • Key Concepts:
    • Aerodynamics
    • Center of Pressure/Gravity
    • Propulsion Systems
    • Structural Design
