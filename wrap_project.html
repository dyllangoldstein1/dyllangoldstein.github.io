Wireless RF & Analog Communication System

An end-to-end project to design, build, and test a 27MHz wireless communication system from the ground up.

Project Overview

The Wireless, RF, and Analog Project (WRAP) is a comprehensive undertaking to build a complete digital communication system without wires. The core goal is to successfully transmit a string of bits from one microcontroller to another over a 27MHz radio frequency link.

This project covers the entire signal chain, from writing the embedded code that encodes digital data into an analog waveform, to designing and building the physical transmitter and receiver hardware, and finally, decoding the analog signal back into an error-free digital format.

Learning Objectives & Key Challenges

A primary challenge will be the **design of the analog RF front-end**. This requires creating a series of sensitive circuits, including RF amplifiers, mixers, and filters, from discrete components. Success depends on careful component selection and achieving stable biasing for all active elements like transistors.

Another key challenge is **maintaining signal integrity**. At 27MHz, factors like impedance matching and minimizing noise become critical. This will involve applying transmission line theory to design matching networks and implementing a thoughtful PCB layout to prevent signal degradation and interference between stages.

Finally, the **digital communications aspect** presents its own hurdles. The task involves developing a robust modulation and encoding scheme in embedded C to ensure that the data can be reliably recovered by the receiver, even in the presence of noise.

Anticipated Outcome & Skills Gained

The final outcome of this project will be a functional pair of custom-built transmitter and receiver boards capable of wireless data transmission. Through this process, I expect to gain deep, hands-on experience in the entire lifecycle of an RF system. Key skills will include circuit simulation in **LTSpice**, PCB layout with **Autodesk Eagle**, firmware development in **Embedded C**, and practical use of RF bench equipment like vector network analyzers and spectrum analyzers for testing and validation.
