555 Timer Synthesizer

An exploration of classic analog components and digital control using an Arduino.

Project Overview

This project merged the digital world of microcontrollers with classic analog electronics. I designed and built a simple synthesizer on a breadboard where an Arduino Nano digitally controls the frequency of a 555 timer, which is configured as an astable multivibrator (an oscillator) to produce sound.

Core Concepts & Implementation

The 555 timer's output frequency is determined by its external timing capacitor and resistors. To achieve digital control, I used the Arduino's PWM output connected to a low-pass filter. This created a variable analog voltage that was fed into the 555 timer's control voltage pin, allowing me to dynamically change the pitch of the output square wave.

Final Outcome & What I Learned

The final result was an interactive synthesizer that could play different notes based on inputs to the Arduino. This project provided great insight into the functionality of the versatile 555 timer IC and gave me practical experience in interfacing a digital microcontroller with an analog circuit to achieve a specific outcome.
